Literature Survey

Take Control of Your Skin Health, Insights from the Latest Research

Skin diseases are becoming a major global threat due to their rapid spread. Skin conditions can progress to a deadly type of cancer called "melanoma" if left untreated. Because of its complexity, melanoma is difficult to diagnose. Therefore, a study proposed a skin cancer detection system using VGG 16, InceptionV3 and ResNet. This computer vision-based methods have been proposed as an alternative, with the potential to provide more accurate diagnosis. In this they have mentioned that a dataset consisting of 10,500 skin cancer images from the International Skin Imaging Collaboration (ISIC) was created by selecting images from 7 different skin cancer categories. Three CNN models, namely VGG16 and I nceptionV3 were trained on this dataset to classify skin cancer. Based on the experimental results, this review validates that the proposed model performs admirably in accurately classifying skin cancer, and the obtained results are acceptable when compared to other modern methods [1].


The research done on skin disease detection using image processing with data mining and DL is mobile based so can be used even in remote areas. The patients needs to provide the image of infected area and it is given as an input to the application. Image processing and DL techniques process it and deliver the most accurate output. They compare two distinct methods for an algorithm that detects skin diseases in real time based on accuracy in this paper. They have compared SVM and CNN. The results of real-time testing are presented [2].

Research Gap

The following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent research.

Multi-Modal Integration

Most research concentrates entirely on image-based detection, ignoring written descriptions of symptoms. This study solves this by merging image and text data to provide a more comprehensive diagnosis.

Personalized Treatment Suggestions

Few studies make treatment recommendations based on diagnosed problems. This study stands out because it combines machine learning algorithms to recommend appropriate treatments based on massive datasets of treatment outcomes and patient reactions.

Treatment Progress Tracker

Most studies do not provide mechanisms for ongoing monitoring of treatment effectiveness, which is crucial for managing chronic skin conditions. This project addresses this gap by introducing the Feedback analysis, which offers continuous feedback based on the comparison of before and after treatment images.

Research Problem & Solution

From Problem to Solution, Advancing Skin Disease Care

- Problem

Despite the advancements in technology and healthcare, there are still challenges in accurately detecting, diagnosing, and treating skin diseases. Traditional methods rely heavily on in-person consultations with dermatologists, which can be time-consuming, expensive, and inaccessible to many individuals, especially in remote areas. Additionally, misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis can lead to ineffective treatments and worsened health outcomes. There is a need for an intelligent system that can efficiently identify skin diseases, provide accurate diagnoses, recommend appropriate treatments, and facilitate patient feedback and monitoring, all within a mobile app platform.


- Solution

The proposed solution offers an innovative approach to addressing the challenges of skin disease detection and management by leveraging state-of-the-art technologies. Through a combination of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Support Vector Machines (SVMs), and advanced image processing techniques, the system ensures accurate classification of skin diseases from user-submitted images. Additionally, by integrating Natural Language Processing (NLP) algorithms, the system extends its diagnostic capabilities beyond image-based classification, enabling analysis of textual descriptions provided by users to enhance disease identification accuracy. Furthermore, the system employs machine learning algorithms to analyze extensive datasets of treatment outcomes, enabling the provision of personalized treatment recommendations based on the diagnosed skin disease. Through feedback analysis, which involves tracking treatment progress and providing users with valuable insights, the system empowers individuals to monitor their skin health effectively and make informed decisions about their treatment journey. Developed as a mobile app using the Flutter framework, the system ensures accessibility across various platforms, offering users convenient access to accurate skin disease detection, diagnosis, personalized treatment recommendations, and feedback analysis. Ultimately, this intelligent system enables users to take proactive measures towards their skin health, leading to improved treatment outcomes and overall well-being.

Research Objectives

Unlocking New Possibilities: Research Goals for Improved Skin Health

1

Image Classification & Diagnosis

The first objective is to accurately identify and classify various types of skin diseases from input images using machine learning techniques. This image classification and diagnosis function will serve as the core component of our system, enabling users to receive preliminary diagnostic information about their skin conditions.



Symptoms Identification and Diagnosis

The second objective is to develop the Symptoms Identification function. Its main aim is to accurately identify and diagnose skin disease symptoms based on text descriptions provided by users. This integration of textual input with image data enhances the system's capability for early detection and treatment of skin conditions.

Methodology

Unveiling the Evidence, Exploring Our Skin Disease Research Methodology

The proposed Intelligent System for Skin Disease prediction consists of 4 main components. They are.


  • (1) Image Classification & Diagnosis

  • (2) Symptoms Identification and Diagnosis

  • (3) Treatment Recommendation

  • (4) Feedback Analyze



Figure 1: depicts the workflow of an ISSDP.

It starts with a user capturing an image of their skin lesion. The system then analyzes the image using a CNN for disease classification, while also processing any user-entered symptoms through an NLP model. Based on the combined analysis, the ISSDP recommends potential treatments from a knowledge base. Finally, the user can upload a follow-up image to compare with the initial one. An SVM model analyzes these images to determine if the condition is improving or worsening, providing feedback to the user and potentially recommending consultation with a dermatologist.


Overall, this research explored CNN, SVM, and NLP in the setting of ISDDS for skin disease detection. The findings obtained in this study are promising, as the approach has proven to be effective in labelling different conditions with high accuracy. However, by addressing the identified limitations and seeking further development, the approach B C D may become a viable alternative for dermatologists worldwide
